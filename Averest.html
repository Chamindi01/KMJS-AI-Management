  • Electric weighing scales are modern devices used for measuring weight accurately and conveniently, using electronic sensors and digital displays. They range from small kitchen scales to large industrial scales and are typically categorized as follows:

    • 1. Kitchen Scales: Small, highly sensitive scales used for measuring food ingredients. These are popular in homes and restaurants.

    2. Bathroom Scales: Used to measure body weight, often featuring added functions like body fat percentage, BMI, and connectivity to health apps.

    3. Industrial Scales: Designed to weigh heavy items, these are used in warehouses and manufacturing plants, often equipped with additional functionalities like data logging and remote control.

    4. Analytical and Precision Scales: Highly accurate scales, often found in laboratories and research settings, used to measure very small weights (up to micrograms).

    5. Retail Scales: Common in grocery stores and markets, these scales often include a label printer for item weights and prices.
